Where It’s Sold

  • Smoke shops
  • Vape stores
  • Gas stations
  • CBD retailers
  • Online head shops
  • Convenience stores

Look For These Clues

Packaging with mushroom imagery, red caps with white spots (Amanita look)
Terms like “muscimol”, “fly agaric”, “legal shrooms”, or “trip”
Gummies, chocolates, or shots with high mushroom extract claims
Bright neon colors, candy themes, or “psychedelic” branding
Products sold next to kratom, THC, or other gas station supplements
Warnings like “not for human consumption” or “research purposes”

Parent Tip

Photograph the front and back of any package before discarding – labels change often.
Ask exactly where and when it was purchased: store name, location, or website.
Look for ingredient lists: Amanita muscaria extract, muscimol, or ibotenic acid.
If your child experiences confusion, agitation, vomiting, or hallucinations, seek medical attention immediately.
