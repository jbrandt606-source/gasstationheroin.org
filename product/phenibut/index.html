Phenibut Product ID Guide

Phenibut is sold as capsules, powders, and energy drinks — often labeled for mood, relaxation, or focus. It is addictive and can cause severe withdrawal.

Look For These Clues

Capsules marketed as mood, focus, or sleep supplements
Bright energy‑drink style packaging with phrases like “extra strength”, “relaxation”, or “anti‑anxiety”
Products combining caffeine and phenibut (e.g., “energy + calm”)
“Nootropic” or “cognitive support” claims on labels
Small bulk powder bags labeled “not for human consumption”
Names like Sleep Walker, Red Dawn, Addall, or Za Za Silver

Parent Tip

Always photograph both sides of the package before throwing it away – labels change frequently.
Ask where and when it was purchased: specific store name, location, or website.
Look for the ingredient list: phenibut, β‑phenyl‑γ‑aminobutyric acid, or Noofen.
If your child experiences anxiety, insomnia, agitation, or withdrawal symptoms, seek medical advice immediately.
