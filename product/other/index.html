Look For These Clues

Terms like “pseudo”, “MGM‑15”, “dihydro”, or “hydroxy” on packaging
Tablet blister packs, gummy pouches, or small glass vials
Bright, candy‑inspired colors and cartoon mascots
Language about “euphoria”, “sedation”, “mood lift”, or “heavy relaxation”
Products displayed right next to kratom, 7‑OH, or vape hardware
Very small text that says “not for human consumption” (common loophole)

Parent Tip

Always photograph the front and back of the package before discarding.
Ask exactly where and when the product was bought — store name, city, online platform.
Check for milligrams per serving and total alkaloid content.
If your child experiences nausea, agitation, or withdrawal‑like symptoms, contact a medical professional immediately.
