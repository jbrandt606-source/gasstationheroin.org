Look For These Clues

Names like Tianaa, Za Za, Pegasus, Neptune’s Fix, or TD Red
Small plastic bottles with capsules, often red/white/green vein marketing
Liquid shots in slim vials, often labeled “elixir” or “tonic”
Claims of “euphoria”, “opioid‑like effects”, “relaxation”, or “mood elevation”
Products displayed near kratom, 7‑OH, and other gas station supplements
Very small print that says “not for human consumption” (common legal disclaimer)

Parent Tip

Photograph the front and back of any package before discarding – labels change often.
Ask exactly where and when it was purchased: specific store name, location, or website.
Look for the ingredient label – tianeptine sodium or tianeptine sulfate may be listed.
If your child experiences nausea, agitation, or withdrawal symptoms, seek medical advice immediately.
