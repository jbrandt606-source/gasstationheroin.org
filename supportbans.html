Support Kratom Bans

Kratom and its active alkaloids are associated with increasing rates of poisonings, hospitalizations, and deaths — yet these products remain unregulated in many states. MAHA supports efforts to restrict the sale and marketing of kratom through statewide bans, scheduling, or emergency actions. Here's why:

Key Harm Statistics

  • CDC SUDORS reported 1,151 kratom-related deaths in 2023 alone.
  • Florida Medical Examiner Commission listed 516 kratom-caused deaths from 2020–2023.
  • NPDS poison centers logged 10,272 kratom exposures from 2014–2023 — 45% required hospitalization.
  • FDA FAERS includes 1,437 adverse events linked to kratom — 707 involved death.
  • FDA CAERS confirms 189 hospitalizations and 188 cases of dependence from kratom products.
Important Policy Note:
Targeting only 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) in regulation is insufficient. The overwhelming majority of kratom-related poisonings, hospitalizations, and deaths are caused by the full kratom plant and its primary alkaloid, mitragynine — not isolated 7-OH. A partial ban would leave the core public health threat untouched.
