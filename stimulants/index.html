Unregulated stimulants are hiding in plain sight as workout supplements and energy boosters.

From high-stim pre-workout with DMAA and DMHA to energy drinks with extreme caffeine and herbal stimulants — these products are sold in every gas station, vape shop, and convenience store. Teens buy them without ID, and the consequences can be deadly: heart attacks, strokes, seizures, and addiction.

Recognize packaging & take action

🚨 Red flags on shelves

  • Pre-workout with names like “Crack,” “Jack3d,” “Condemned,” “Addall” — or ingredients DMAA/DMHA
  • Energy drinks with 200–300mg caffeine per can — especially if sold next to alcohol
  • Energy shots at checkout counters — tiny bottles with massive stimulant loads
  • “Proprietary blends” that hide exact stimulant amounts
  • No age verification — sold to anyone, including teens

🛡️ How you can fight back

  • Report DMAA/DMHA products to FDA MedWatch and the FTC
  • Ask gas station owners to check IDs for stimulant products
  • Warn schools, sports coaches, and parent groups
  • Advocate for age restrictions on high-caffeine stimulants
