What is Gas Station Heroin?

Gas station heroin is a term for dangerous, unregulated drug‑like products sold legally in vape shops, smoke shops, and convenience stores. These products often contain kratom, tianeptine, or synthetic cannabinoids and are marketed with bright packaging next to candy and energy drinks.

14,449+ Exposures

Reported to U.S. poison centers in recent years involving these products.

1,151 Deaths

With mitragynine (kratom) detected in 2023 alone.

304 Commercial Products

7‑OH products identified on shelves targeting unaware consumers.

Child‑Appealing Packaging

Over 80 products found with bright colors, cartoons, or candy‑like names.

How to protect your family: Learn the names, recognize the packaging, talk to your teens, and report local stores. Early conversation saves lives.

For immediate help, call Poison Control (1-800-222-1222) or text HOME to 741741.
