Sedatives

Phenibut, Kava, and Blue Lotus extracts are sold legally beside candy and vapes — potent, unregulated sedatives with real addiction and health risks.

See the Dangers Protect Your Family

Gas station sedatives are hiding in plain sight — as sleep gummies, energy shots, and “relaxation” tonics.

From phenibut (a powerful GABAergic sedative) to kava (linked to liver toxicity) and blue lotus (a psychoactive plant sold in vapes and candies), these unregulated products are marketed as harmless supplements. In reality, they cause sedation, dependence, withdrawal, and dangerous drug interactions.

Recognize packaging & take action

🚨 Red flags on shelves

  • “Relax-All,” “Red Dawn,” “Sleep Walk,” “Feel Free,” “Silly Kava,” “Stoned,” “Lotus Kush”
  • Gummies, vapes, or shots claiming “stress relief,” “deep sleep,” or “plant medicine”
  • No age verification requirement — sold next to candy and energy drinks
  • Packages that look like wellness products but contain psychoactive sedatives
  • Combination products: kava + kratom, phenibut + caffeine, blue lotus + mushrooms

🛡️ How you can fight back

  • Report suspicious products to FDA MedWatch and the FTC (deceptive marketing)
  • Share photos with local health department & school boards
  • Advocate for state bans on unregulated sedatives like phenibut and high-dose kava extracts
  • Use this site to warn parent groups, PTA, and community coalitions
