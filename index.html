“Gas Station Heroin” is Kratom — and its 7-OH metabolite drives the risk.

Kratom is sold in gas stations and vape shops as a “natural” mood or energy product. Its active compounds — mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) — act at opioid receptors, creating dependence and withdrawal. Learn the facts below.

What is Kratom?

Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) is a Southeast Asian plant used in powders, capsules, and shots. It’s marketed as a supplement but can mimic opioid effects. No approved medical use; no dosage standards; no consistent labeling.

What is 7-OH?

7-Hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) is a potent metabolite of kratom. Even small amounts can create tolerance and withdrawal. Some products advertise “7-OH” content — a red flag for high potency and risk.

7-OH & Kratom Risks

Adverse Events

FDA and CDC data link kratom to hospitalizations, deaths, and severe withdrawal symptoms. MAHA’s data pages summarize thousands of FAERS reports and poison-center calls.

Packaging & Sales

Kratom products are often sold next to energy drinks with colorful labels like “K-Shot.” Some include synthetic additives or claim “lab-tested” potency that isn’t verified by any regulatory agency.
