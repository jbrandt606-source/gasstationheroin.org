Know the real danger

Hidden chemicals, unknown doses

Products labeled as "herbal incense," "glass cleaner," or "research chemicals" often contain tianeptine (gas station heroin), bromazolam, etizolam, or unregulated kratom extracts. These cause respiratory depression, seizures, and sudden death.

  • ⚠️ Tianeptine: highly addictive, withdrawal similar to opioids
  • ⚠️ Unregulated vapes: heavy metals, vitamin E acetate, synthetic cannabinoids
  • ⚠️ Nitrites / poppers: vision loss, cardiac events

What parents & communities can do

Gas stations and smoke shops often avoid age verification. Advocacy, store reporting, and local ordinances can shut down dangerous sales. We provide toolkits for school boards and law enforcement.

  • 📋 Spot warning signs: tiny colorful vials, "not for human consumption" labels
  • 🏛️ Petition local cities to ban gas station synthetic drugs
  • 📢 Share our fact sheets with parent-teacher associations

Spot the signs — protect your child

In the store

Look for packaging that mimics energy drinks but contains tianeptine ("gas station heroin"), kratom shots behind the counter, or vape cartridges with homemade labels. Many products include QR codes that lead to grey-market dosing info.

Physical warning signs

Unexplained drowsiness, pinpoint pupils, sudden mood swings, secretive behavior, and unusual powders or vaping devices. Emergency rooms report teens with seizures after using "gas station special" blends.
