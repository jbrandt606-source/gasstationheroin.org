Know the real danger

Hidden chemicals, unknown doses

Products labeled as "herbal incense" or "glass cleaner" often contain tianeptine (gas station heroin), bromazolam, or unregulated kratom extracts. These cause respiratory depression, seizures, and sudden death.

  • ⚠️ Tianeptine: highly addictive, opioid-like withdrawal
  • ⚠️ Unregulated vapes: heavy metals, synthetic cannabinoids
  • ⚠️ Nitrites / poppers: vision loss, cardiac events

What parents & communities can do

Gas stations and smoke shops often avoid age verification. Advocacy, store reporting, and local ordinances can shut down dangerous sales. We provide toolkits for school boards and law enforcement.

  • 📋 Spot warning signs: "not for human consumption" labels
  • 🏛️ Petition local bans on synthetic drugs
  • 📢 Share fact sheets with parent-teacher associations

Spot the signs — protect your child

In the store

Packaging that mimics energy drinks but contains tianeptine, kratom shots behind the counter, vape cartridges with homemade labels. Many include QR codes to grey-market dosing.

Physical warning signs

Unexplained drowsiness, pinpoint pupils, sudden mood swings, secretive behavior, unusual powders or devices. ERs report seizures after "gas station special" use.
