What inhalant products are sold in gas stations and vape shops?

Two common categories are poppers (alkyl nitrites sold as “leather cleaner,” “room odorizer,” or “solvent cleaner”) and nitrous oxide (sold as whipped cream chargers, “Galaxy Gas,” “Miami Magic,” or flavored canisters). Both are often sold with disclaimers like “not for human consumption,” but their labeling, flavors, and placement make the intended use clear.

Educational resources for parents

What to look for on shelves

  • Small glass or plastic bottles labeled “leather cleaner,” “room odorizer,” “solvent cleaner” — brand names like Jungle Juice, Rush, Super Rush, Hell Fire, Speed, Ecstasy Pop
  • Metal canisters (8g–10g) or larger tanks labeled “whipped cream charger,” “Galaxy Gas,” “Miami Magic,” “N2O infusion system” — especially flavored (cotton candy, aloe grape)
  • Products sold near registers or in glass display cases, often with no age restriction signs

How to talk with teens

  • Explain that “not for human consumption” is a legal loophole — not a safety guarantee
  • Discuss risks: poppers can cause blood pressure drops, fainting, methemoglobinemia; nitrous can cause nerve damage with heavy use (B12 inactivation)
  • Explain why mixing with Viagra/Cialis (poppers) or using nitrous from tanks can be especially dangerous
  • Encourage questions about packaging that uses candy flavors or drug‑like names
