Report Dangerous Products

See Something.
Say Something.

Gas stations, vape shops, and convenience stores sometimes sell unregulated, drug-like products. Your report can protect families and hold sellers accountable.

Start Here

1. What to document

The more information you have, the more helpful your report will be.

Photos of the product

Take clear photos of the front and back of the package. Capture ingredient labels, warnings, and any dosage information.

Store details

Write down the store name, full address, date, and time of your visit. Note the exact shelf or display location.

Receipt or price tag

Include photos of receipts, shelf tags, or price stickers. This helps investigators verify where and when the product was sold.

3. Red flags to watch for

Be alert to products and marketing that put people at risk.

  • Sold as a "research chemical"

    Report facts, not accusations. Stick to what you saw and have evidence for.

  • Claims to cure, treat, or prevent disease

    Unproven health claims are a major warning sign. Include photographs when possible.

  • No ingredient list or hidden ingredients

    Products that avoid transparency are often unregulated and dangerous.

  • Marketed next to candy, snacks, or energy drinks

    Targeting youth with bright packaging and sweet flavors is a common tactic.
