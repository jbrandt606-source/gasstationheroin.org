What is Kratom? What is 7-OH?

What is Kratom?

Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa) is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. Its leaves contain psychoactive alkaloids, primarily mitragynine, which interacts with opioid receptors in the brain. Kratom is sold as a powder, capsule, liquid, or resin extract in gas stations, vape shops, and online. At low doses, it may act as a stimulant. At higher doses, it can produce opioid-like sedation and carry risks of tolerance, dependence, and withdrawal. It is not approved by the FDA for any use, and has been associated with seizures, liver toxicity, and death.

What is 7-OH?

7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) is a metabolite of mitragynine — and a much more potent opioid agonist. Some kratom products are spiked with synthetic 7-OH or marketed to highlight its presence, making them significantly riskier. 7-OH can lead to respiratory depression, overdose, and hospitalizations. Some formulations sold as K-shot, OPMS, or 7Tabz have been linked to serious adverse events requiring naloxone reversal.

Why a 7-OH Ban Isn’t Enough

Most kratom-related deaths and poisonings involve plain kratom powders or capsules — not just the 7-OH-enriched products. The body naturally converts mitragynine into 7-OH during liver metabolism, especially when kratom is brewed into teas or reduced into resin extracts. Restricting only 7-OH leaves the unregulated kratom industry free to sell opioid-class alkaloids without safety controls.
